“Open with DiemDesk” (like Gmail shows): once you install DiemDesk (it’s a PWA), it registers as a handler — on Android it appears in Gmail’s Share sheet, on desktop Chrome/Edge it becomes an “Open with” option for .pdf. A plain browser tab can’t do this — it needs the install. The native app (roadmap) completes iPhone + full open-with.

Why the viewer, and how free/Pro works here

  • 1
    The viewer is FREE. Viewing on-device (pages, zoom, search, thumbnails) — the acquisition hook and a huge search term (“pdf viewer online / open pdf”).
  • 2
    “Do more” is a hand-off, not a paywall. Most actions open a free tool (compress, split, sign, fill, watermark, to-Word). A few are Pro and clearly badged (Redact, PDF→Excel, OCR, AI, Vault) — same gating we use across the site.
  • 3
    The moat: the file never re-uploads between steps — view → act → act again, all on your device. That’s the “one private workspace” story rivals (server-first) can’t match.
  • 4
    Build order: viewer first (pdf.js infra already exists) → PWA share_target/file_handlers for open-with → native apps for iOS.
