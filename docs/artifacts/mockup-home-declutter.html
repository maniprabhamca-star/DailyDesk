Home tools section — from a wall to a shop window

Today the home page lists every tool as an equal tile (~65 of them) — a wall that makes 65 feel like clutter instead of power. The proposal: a sticky category chip-bar that jumps to each group, every group capped at its 6 most-used tools + a "See all →" into the full /tools directory, and the AI suite presented as one distinct violet block. The home page becomes a shop window; /tools stays the warehouse.

Design notes. (1) Curation, not amputation: nothing is removed — every tool keeps its page and lives in /tools; the home page just stops treating "Word counter" and "Compress PDF" as equals. (2) The chip-bar sticks under the header while scrolling the section — on mobile it scrolls horizontally, replacing 65 tiles of scrolling with 7 chips. (3) The "+N more" tile at the end of each row is a second, in-flow door to /tools — people click the row-end tile far more than a header link. (4) Featured picks per group are data-driven (from the usage beacon's tool-open counts, hand-adjustable), so the shop window rearranges itself as real usage shifts. (5) The AI block keeps the violet family accent and honestly badges "launching with Pro" — it advertises the Pro tier without a single fake "free" tile. (6) Tile microcopy stays benefit-first ("under 200 KB", "page-cited") per the house style.
